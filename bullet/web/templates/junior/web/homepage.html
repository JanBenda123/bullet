What is Náboj?

About Náboj

Náboj is a team competition in math and physics, 4 team members. This year the competition takes place on 25.10.2022 10:00-12:00

Competing sites

International competition held in Croatia (online), Czech republic, France (online), Netherlands (online), Poland and Slovakia.

Problem set

Teams have 2 hours to solve as many problems as possible. We do not require the participants to remember the formulas, they will receive a cheatsheet.

Rewards

Successful contestants receive fabulous prices and endless fame.

Knowledge-based competition with enormous amount of fun

Naboj Junior is an international math & physics competition designed for teams of four pupils in 4th class that represent their school. The competition lasts 120 minutes during which the teams try to solve as many problems as possible. At the beginning of the competition each team receives six problems. They receive a new one as soon as the team correctly solves any of the problems. The team that solves most problems correctly in the given time limit takes the victory. The best teams in each region receive certificates and material prizes.

Various problems that fit everyone

The difficulty of the problems is adjusted to the knowledge of pupils in fourth class. The problems are in ascending order of their perceived difficulty. Thanks to this, the competition is equally appropriate for students who are inexperienced in mathematical competitions and also those, who have already succeeded in Mathematical Olympiad.

Problems in Naboj differ considerably from the routine school exercises which usually require only direct application of standard methods. We are trying to include problems that require a certain amount of inventiveness and ingenuity.

Success in the competition depends not only on the individual abilities of the team members but also on their efficient cooperation. Apart from enhancing imagination and logical reasoning, our goal is to attract people to the inexhaustible beauty hidden in mathematics and physics.

Compare yourself with students from foreign countries

Naboj Junior will be held in hybrid format for the first time. Some of competing countries will held the competition online, therefore the pupils will be able to compete only via our online system. Other countries will held the competition in the original on-site format, where teams register for the competition in the closest local competing site. These local sites are ensured by high school students (volunteers). The problem set is the same for each competing site.

Altogether countries participating in Naboj Junior are: Croatia, Czech republic, France, Netherlands, Poland and Slovakia. The competition is evaluated in each country separately, but pupils can compare themselves on international level in final results.
